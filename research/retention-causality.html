Causal Modeling of User Retention with Large Language Models

Integrating LLM-based behavioral signals with heterogeneous treatment effect estimation
Presented at INFORMS 2025; Preparing for Marketing Science submission

Abstract

User retention is a critical challenge for digital platforms. While many predictive approaches exist, few explain why and for whom retention interventions succeed. This paper introduces a unified causal modeling framework that integrates large language models (LLMs) with heterogeneous treatment effect estimation to uncover the mechanisms behind retention.

We extract psychological signals from user-generated text—sentiment, identity activation, cognitive complexity—and embed them into quasi-experimental designs (difference-in-differences, causal forests). Using a large-scale natural experiment, we find that identity-framed notifications significantly increase retention, with effects strongly moderated by language-derived user traits. Mediation analysis shows that identity activation and cognitive engagement explain over 60% of the treatment effect.

Our findings advance marketing science by demonstrating how LLM-based behavioral features enhance causal inference, offering actionable strategies for personalized platform interventions.

Key Findings

Identity-framed notifications significantly increase user retention

Language-derived user traits strongly moderate treatment effects

Identity activation and cognitive engagement explain over 60% of the treatment effect

LLM-based behavioral features enhance causal inference capabilities
