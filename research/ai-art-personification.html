Eliza Tang

Examining the Communication Effectiveness of Artificial Intelligence Art Personification

Status: Published and Presented at IAMCR 2023

Abstract

Artificial intelligence (AI) art is an emerging product of the computer age, and AI artworks now increasingly exhibit distinct personalities. Based on the theoretical perspective of anthropomorphic communication, this study combines computational visual technology with manual coding to analyze 40 AI artists' works and explore the communication effects of anthropomorphized AI art.

Findings reveal that AI-generated artworks create an "anthropomorphic effect" in visual art, triggering emotional empathy and relational identification with audiences. The anthropomorphism of visual language symbols and spatial presentation significantly enhances communication effectiveness, while differences in the degree of anthropomorphism do not always translate into stronger communication outcomes.

The study enriches theories of visual language, semiotics, and humanization trends in media, while offering practical insights for AI art creators. By integrating real-time cultural contexts and symbolic visual strategies, AI artists can enhance communicative impact and audience engagement.

Keywords: Visual culture, AI art, personification, anthropomorphic communication, media semiotics

Methods

  • Data: 40 high-value AI artworks collected from leading platforms (Art Blocks, SuperRare, Nifty Gateway, etc.)
  • Techniques: Combination of computational vision (Face++, Baidu AI) and manual content coding
  • Variables: Language symbol personification, visual symbol personification (body language, spatial presentation), transaction amounts
  • Analysis: Multiple regression to test the impact of personification features on communication and dissemination effects

Results (Highlights)

  • Language symbols: Use of slang and colloquial expressions significantly enhanced communication impact
  • Visual symbols: Spatial presentation (shapes, lens distance, visual angles) strongly influenced dissemination (measured by sales/transactions)
  • Unexpected finding: Body language and emotional dynamism did not significantly improve communication effects, contrasting with prior literature
  • Implication: Visual anthropomorphism and symbolic framing are more critical than motion or expression richness in AI art dissemination
