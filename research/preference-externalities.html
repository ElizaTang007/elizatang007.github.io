Preference Externalities Research

Modeling willingness-to-pay shifts under social information on digital platforms

Research Question: How do peer behaviors and social signals influence individual preference formation and willingness-to-pay in digital marketplaces?

Background & Motivation

Digital platforms create complex ecosystems where user preferences are not formed in isolation. Social information, peer behaviors, and platform signals create externalities that systematically shift individual willingness-to-pay. Understanding these mechanisms is crucial for:

  • Platform design and optimization
  • Fair pricing strategies
  • Regulatory policy development
  • User welfare maximization

Policy Implications

This research informs several critical policy areas:

  • Platform Regulation: Evidence for transparency requirements in recommendation systems
  • Consumer Protection: Framework for identifying and mitigating preference manipulation
  • Competition Policy: Understanding of network effects in digital markets
