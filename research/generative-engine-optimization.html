Simulating Search and Strategy: Causal Evaluation of Generative Engine Optimization via Synthetic Respondents

Combining synthetic respondents with causal inference to evaluate GEO strategies
Targeting IJRM Special Issue on Generative AI & Synthetic Data (Deadline: May 31, 2026)

Abstract

Generative AI is reshaping search and marketing optimization, yet its causal impact on user behavior remains underexplored. This paper proposes a framework that combines synthetic respondents with causal inference to evaluate Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) strategies.

We generate controlled marketing content using LLMs and construct synthetic user cohorts to simulate search and recommendation responses. Quasi-experimental methods (DiD, causal forests, uplift modeling) estimate the marginal effects of different content framings. Validation against real platform data shows that synthetic cohorts can effectively predict user engagement trends and reveal heterogeneity in treatment effects.

Our results highlight the value of synthetic data for low-risk, scalable marketing experiments. The study contributes methodologically by integrating synthetic respondents with causal evaluation, and practically by offering firms a blueprint for testing GEO strategies before real-world deployment.

Key Findings

Synthetic cohorts can effectively predict user engagement trends

Quasi-experimental methods reveal heterogeneity in treatment effects

Validation against real platform data confirms synthetic data reliability

Low-risk, scalable marketing experiments using synthetic respondents

Blueprint for testing GEO strategies before real-world deployment
