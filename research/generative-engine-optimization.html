Generative Engine Optimization

Simulating search and marketing strategies via synthetic respondents and causal inference frameworks
Target: IJRM Special Issue on Generative AI & Synthetic Data (Deadline: May 2026)

Abstract

As generative AI transforms search and marketing landscapes, traditional optimization approaches struggle to capture the dynamic interactions between synthetic content, user behavior, and platform algorithms. This research introduces a novel framework for Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) that leverages synthetic respondents and causal inference to simulate and optimize search and marketing strategies.

We develop a comprehensive simulation environment that generates realistic user interactions with AI-powered search engines and marketing platforms. Using causal inference methods, we identify the key drivers of engagement and conversion in synthetic environments, enabling the prediction of real-world performance without extensive A/B testing.

Our approach combines large language models with behavioral economics principles to create synthetic respondents that exhibit realistic search patterns, preferences, and decision-making processes. Through counterfactual analysis, we demonstrate how different content strategies, algorithmic changes, and user interface modifications affect engagement metrics.

This research contributes to the emerging field of synthetic data in marketing by providing a scalable framework for testing and optimizing generative AI applications in search and marketing contexts.

Research Contributions

Novel GEO Framework for optimizing generative AI in search and marketing

Synthetic Respondent Generation using LLMs and behavioral economics

Causal Inference Methods for identifying key engagement drivers

Scalable Simulation Environment for testing without extensive A/B testing

Counterfactual Analysis for predicting real-world performance
